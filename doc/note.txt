 GROUPOID INFINITY: INTERNAL LANGUAGES, CATEGORICAL MODELS,
 AND THE SPECTRAL LATTICE OF FORMAL SYSTEMS
================================================================================
Groupoid Infinity

Abstract:
In this article, we investigate the conjecture that no single formal language can
serve as a universal foundation for the entirety of mathematics, proposing instead
that mathematics is best served by a system of specialized languages connected by
functorial transports. We analyze the Groupoid Infinity stack of formal languages
as a LATTICE of internal languages, rather than a linear tower. The languages fall
into three distinct branches: a constructive type theory and set analysis spine
(Henk–Frank–Christine), a super homotopy spine extending to differential cohesion
and quantum spectra (Henk–Per–Anders–Urs), and an infinity category theory spine
(Dan–Mike–Ulrik). Due to their divergent semantic and syntactic structures,
attempting to unify them into a single monolithic abstract syntax tree (AST) is
often impractical and conceptually strained. We demonstrate how these separate
domains interact functorially, construct syntactic CW-complexes and chain complexes
for each language individually, define their cohomology groups, and assemble a
spectral sequence over the full lattice of language categories, providing a
rigorous mathematical framework for this multi-language foundation.

1. Categorical Foundations
================================================================================
* Categories with Families (CwF):
  A CwF consists of a category C of contexts and substitutions with a terminal 
  object, equipped with a functor Ty : C^op → Set, a presheaf of terms 
  Tm : (∫ Ty)^op → Set, and a context comprehension operation with projection morphisms.

* Natural Models (Awodey):
  A category C with terminal object 1 and a representable natural transformation
  p : Tm → Ty between presheaves. Pullbacks of p along Yoneda provide context extension.

* Grothendieck Fibration:
  A functor p : E → B is a fibration when for every X ∈ E and u : J → p(X)
  in B there exists a Cartesian lift f : Y → X in E.

The category of languages L has formal languages as objects and syntactic
functors (compilers, type checkers, normalizers, extractors) as morphisms.

2.2 The Semantic Foundations: Structural, Cohesive, and Directed Modalities
The lattice stratifies into structural, cohesive, linear, and directed semantics. The foundational correspondences between semantic models and their internal syntaxes progressively build up structure:

* Structural Core: At the base, Locally Cartesian Closed Categories (LCCC) provide the semantics for MLTT, the pure structural type theory. Upgrading the semantics to oo-categories adds globular path equality, yielding Cubical Type Theory with Path types but without Higher Inductive Types (HITs). Finally, a full oo-topos models Cubical Type Theory with HITs, which maintains a very lightweight core and syntax.
* Cohesive Modalities: Cohesive Type Theory (Felix) is developed specifically to model modalities such as connectedness, compactness, and infinitesimal shapes. Differential Cohesive Type Theory acts as the internal language for Differential Cohesive oo-topoi, adding extra structure to oo-topoi just as oo-categories add path equality to LCCCs. These are typically modeled in the base library via undefined axioms (see cohesivett).
* Linear/Tensor Modalities: Symmetric Monoidal Categories (SMC) supply the semantics for resource-sensitive and directed computations. Here, Process Calculus and Interaction Nets (Lafont) act as the internal language, representing modality types with special comonadic spawn arrows that hide the underlying run-time implementation.
* Directed and Simplicial Topoi: Moving toward spatial and temporal operational algebras, presheaf categories (Pres) provide the semantics for Guarded Type Theory (Dan). Incorporating strict directional structures leads to 1-Cat, acting as the semantics for Directed Type Theory (Mike). Finally, scaling to higher dimensions, (oo, 1)-Cat serves as the model for Simplicial Type Theory (Ulrik / Riehl-Shulman STT), extending directed paths with a Segal-like directed interval.

This establishes a rich hierarchy of correspondences:

     [LCCC]               <──────(Internal Language)──────> [MLTT]
     [PiW-Pretopos]        <──────(Internal Language)──────> [MLTT-80 (W-types)]
     [oo-Cat]             <──────(Internal Language)──────> [Cubical (Paths, no HITs)]
     [oo-Topos]           <──────(Internal Language)──────> [Cubical (with HITs)]
     [Cohesive oo-Topos]  <──────(Internal Language)──────> [Cohesive HoTT]
     [SMC]                <──────(Internal Language)──────> [Process Calculus / Interaction Nets]
     [Pres]               <──────(Internal Language)──────> [Guarded Type Theory (Dan)]
     [1-Cat]              <──────(Internal Language)──────> [Directed Type Theory (Mike)]
     [(oo, 1)-Cat]        <──────(Internal Language)──────> [Simplicial Type Theory (Ulrik)]

* The 4-Layer Architecture:
  The sublanguages represent a progressive structure for formalizing mathematical and physical concepts:
  1. Urs.Homotopy: Foundation for homotopical properties of spaces, path types, glue types, and Eilenberg-MacLane spaces.
  2. Urs.SupSmth: Graded universes and tensors, group actions, and super-modality operators for geometric cohesion and differential structure—bosonic/fermionic grades, flat/sharp modalities.
  3. Urs.DiffK: Homotopical foundations via spectra and groupoids, supporting differential K-theory with differential forms, connections, and refined cohomology structures.
  4. Urs.Quantum: Configuration spaces and braids for quantum statistics, supporting quantum systems and linear types (!A) for resource-sensitive quantum programming.
  Each sublanguage culminates in a system tailored to formalize superpoints (ℝ^{m|n}), supersymmetry, and equivariant structures.

6. Cohomology of Programming Languages
================================================================================
Each language's syntax is analyzed INDIVIDUALLY as a CW-complex.

For a formal language O_x, we associate a chain complex of abelian groups (free Z-modules) C_*(O_x) constructed from the syntax and reduction/conversion semantics. This construction models the syntactic space as a cell complex (CW-complex) where paths and higher homotopies encode the computational rules.

* Syntactic CW-Complex:
  For a language O_x with term syntax T generated by a signature Σ, the syntactic cell complex C_*(O_x) is defined in low dimensions by:
  - 0-cells (C_0(O_x)): The free Z-module generated by the set of basic syntactic constructors c ∈ Σ (e.g., Var, Pi, Lam, App for Henk). Any term t ∈ T has an associated constructor-occurrence representation:
    [t] = ∑_{c ∈ Σ} count(c, t) · c  ∈ C_0(O_x)
    where count(c, t) is the number of times the constructor c appears in the syntax tree of t.
    
  - 1-cells (C_1(O_x)): The free Z-module generated by the rewriting and reduction rules r ∈ R of the language (e.g., β-reductions, evaluation steps). Each 1-cell r : t → t' represents a directed rewrite path from a redex term t to a contractum term t'.
    
  - 2-cells (C_2(O_x)): The free Z-module generated by equivalence relations, critical pairs, confluence diagrams, and conversion equations (e.g., η-conversions, commuting conversions). A 2-cell e represents a coherence condition or a closed loop of rewrite steps.
    
  - Higher cells (C_k(O_x) for k ≥ 3): Coherence relations between equations, such as Mac Lane pentagon relations, path-type univalence coherences (in Anders), or quadraticality constraints (in Mike).

* Boundary Operators:
  The boundary operators ∂_k : C_k(O_x) → C_{k-1}(O_x) are defined as follows:
  1. The 1-boundary operator ∂_1 : C_1(O_x) → C_0(O_x) is the linear map determined by the net change in constructor counts caused by a rewrite rule r : t → t':
     ∂_1(r) = [t'] - [t]
     
  2. The 2-boundary operator ∂_2 : C_2(O_x) → C_1(O_x) is determined by the boundary of confluence diagrams or conversion loops. If a 2-cell e represents a confluence diagram from a term s reducing to s' via two different paths of rewrites p_1 = (r_{1,1}, ..., r_{1,m}) and p_2 = (r_{2,1}, ..., r_{2,n}), the boundary is:
     ∂_2(e) = ∑_{j=1..m} r_{1,j} - ∑_{k=1..n} r_{2,k}
     Since both paths p_1 and p_2 start at s and end at s', the net constructor changes must match:
     ∂_1(p_1) = [s'] - [s] = ∂_1(p_2)
     Thus:
     ∂_1(∂_2(e)) = ∂_1(p_1) - ∂_1(p_2) = 0
     This proves that ∂_1 ∘ ∂_2 = 0, establishing a valid chain complex.

* Syntactic Cohomology:
  Let C_*(O_x) be the syntactic chain complex of O_x and let G be an abelian group of coefficients. The cochain complex C^*(O_x; G) = Hom(C_*(O_x), G) has coboundary operators δ^k : C^k(O_x; G) → C^{k+1}(O_x; G) defined by δ^k(φ) = φ ∘ ∂_{k+1}.
  The syntactic cohomology groups are:
  H^k(O_x; G) = ker(δ^k) / im(δ^{k-1})

* Physical Interpretations of Cohomology Groups:
  The cohomology groups H^k(O_x; G) measure fundamental properties of the syntax and semantics of O_x:
  - H^0(O_x; G): Measures the syntactic dimension or independent constructor classes. A class in H^0 is a function f : C_0 → G such that for any rewrite r : t → t', f([t]) = f([t']). Thus, H^0 captures invariants preserved under computation (such as type preservation or conserved weights of terms).
  
  - H^1(O_x; G): Measures normalization obstructions. An element in H^1 is represented by a 1-cocycle (an assignment of weights to rewrites that sums to zero on closed loops) modulo those coming from constructor valuations. Non-trivial H^1 classes indicate non-confluent rewrites, cycles of reductions (non-termination), or irreversible compilation steps.
  
  - H^2(O_x; G): Measures coherence obstructions. It captures the obstructions to filling confluence diagrams with higher-dimensional conversion cells. A non-trivial class in H^2 represents a critical pair (or confluence loop) that cannot be closed by conversions or equivalence cells, pointing to deep semantic mismatches or failures of univalence/coherence.

* Examples across the Branches:

  - Branch I/II: Constructive & Homotopical Spine
    * Henk (O_Π): The core type theory.
      C_0 = { Var, Universe, Pi, Lam, App }
      The 1-cells are generated by β-reduction:
      r_β : App(Lam(x, A, t), u) → t[u/x]
      ∂_1(r_β) = [t[u/x]] - ( App + Lam + [t] + [u] )
      The 2-cells consist of η-conversions:
      e_η : Lam(x, A, App(t, x)) ≡_η t
      Since Henk is strongly normalizing and confluent (Church-Rosser), the higher cohomology H^k(O_Π; Z) for k ≥ 1 is trivial, reflecting a contractible syntactic space.
      
    * Anders (O_Anders): Adds univalence and Glue types. The univalence axiom represents a path-space equivalence between isomorphic types. This introduces higher-dimensional coherences (Glue cells) acting as 3-cells, preventing non-trivial 2-dimensional obstructions.
    
    * Urs (O_Urs): Adds modalities (flat, sharp) and differential structures (forms, connections). The syntactic CW-complex of O_Urs is enriched with topological and differential cells, yielding non-trivial H^k corresponding to de Rham cohomology and Chern-Weil invariants of smooth/differential type structures.

  - Branch III: Operational & Directed Branch
    * Dan (O_Dan): Operational algebra. Here, term syntax encodes algebraic operations (e.g., face maps ∂_i, degeneracies s_i). The cell complex C_*(O_Dan) is isomorphic to the standard simplicial chain complex of the underlying category/algebraic presentation.
    
    * Mike (O_Mike): Directed category theory. 1-cells are non-reversible functors/morphisms, meaning paths are directed. The boundary operator ∂_1 tracks directed change. Quadraticality constraints act as 2-cells that model the non-commutative relation profiles of monoidal/directed types.
    
    * Ulrik (O_Ulrik): Simplicial ∞-categories. Generalizes Mike's 1-categories to directed simplicial spaces. The higher cells (C_k for k ≥ 2) represent the Segal completeness conditions and Rezk completeness conditions, ensuring that the directed paths compose coherently up to higher homotopies.

7. Spectral Sequence of Language Categories
================================================================================
Unlike a simple linear filtration, the language category L is organized as a 
LATTICE (a partially ordered set I) with multiple join-paths. To compute the 
cohomology of the total language L from its sublanguages, we can employ the 
Bousfield-Kan spectral sequence for the homotopy colimit over the poset category I:

    E₂^{p,q} = H^p(I; H^q)  ===>  H^{p+q}(L)

where H^q : I -> Ab is the functor mapping each language O to its cohomology H^q(O).

    E₁^{p,q} = H^{p+q}(F_p / F_{p-1})  ===>  H^{p+q}(L)

* Deep Dive: The Lift Functor (Dan -> Mike / Ulrik)
  The Lift functor acts as a semantical compilation bridge that transports
  combinatorial presentations from the operational layer Dan to the directed
  synthetic type theories of Mike and Ulrik. Rather than a simple syntax
  translator, it elaborates discrete presentations into rich categorical models
  in four distinct phases:
  1. Signature Ingestion: Reads the generator set, face maps, degeneracy maps,
     and algebraic presentation relations directly from the Dan syntax.
  2. Semantical Lifting: Maps the ingested signature into category-theoretic
     structures, translating generator equations into functors, categories,
     and sheaves.
  3. Elaboration to STT: Translates composition equations to identity paths
     (EId) in Ulrik. The directed interval I-> is employed to model the
     domains and boundary conditions.
  4. Metatheory Absorption: Automatically equips the lifted object with the
     abstract category-theoretic library of Ulrik (including Yoneda lemmas,
     limits, adjunctions, and Kan extensions).

* Deep Dive: The Groupoid Core (Ulrik -> Anders)
  The translation from Ulrik to Anders is a structural forgetful conversion
  that extracts the underlying homotopy type (the infinity-groupoid of
  isomorphisms) from a directed infinity-category. Since the simplicial
  directed interval I-> and directed hom-types (x -> y) of Ulrik do not exist
  in the cubical undirected world of Anders, the functor operates as follows:
  - Discards directed arrows, keeping only the sub-groupoid containing
    morphisms with valid inverses (isomorphisms).
  - Discards the Segal and Rezk completeness conditions, transforming directed
    category composition into symmetric path composition.
  - Replaces the directed interval I-> with the cubical symmetric interval I
    (with connection and reversal operations).
